Bmadcalculator — Design Direction Exploration

Three layout approaches for grade 3 tablet calculator · Open in browser to compare

Direction 1
"Classic Column" Recommended
Operators in a right column. Digit pad 3×3 on the left. Most familiar to adults — parents recognise it immediately. Operators are always visible without scanning.
47 + 36 =
83

Strengths

  • Parent-recognisable layout
  • Operators always in view
  • Digits and operators clearly separated by position
  • Equals easy to find (bottom-centre)

Watch-outs

  • Operator column is slim — tap targets slightly narrower
  • Equals shares a row with oops and 0 — crowded bottom row
Direction 2
"Operators First"
Four operator buttons in a prominent top row. Equals is a large double-height button on the right. This hierarchy says "choose your operation first" — matches how Grade 3 teachers explain maths.
63 − 28 =
35

Strengths

  • Operators are visually dominant — the differentiator is front and centre
  • Equals is a very large target — can't miss it
  • Clear visual hierarchy: pick operation → enter numbers → equals

Watch-outs

  • Unconventional — parents may find it unfamiliar
  • Operator row may feel disconnected from digit entry flow
Direction 3
"Equals Dominant"
Full-width equals button spans the entire bottom. Operators integrated into the digit grid. The equals button is unmissable — every calculation ends at the same prominent target.
8 × 7 =
56

Strengths

  • Equals is absolutely unmissable — spans full width
  • Clean visual rhythm — grid + single bottom action
  • Operators integrated naturally into grid flow

Watch-outs

  • Operators lose distinct shape identity — squashed into grid
  • Operator position less predictable (scattered in grid)
  • Oops can be confused with operator buttons nearby

Evaluation Guide

When choosing a direction, consider these questions from the perspective of an 8-year-old using a tablet:

Layout Clarity Can Jamie tell at a glance what to tap first, second, and last?
Operator Confidence Are the four operators clearly distinct from each other and from digits?
Equals Prominence Is the equals button the most visually prominent interactive element?
Parent Trust Would Alex look at this for 5 seconds and immediately understand it?
